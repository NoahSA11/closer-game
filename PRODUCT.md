# Product

## Register

brand+product

## Users

Couples (romantic partners) playing together on one shared device. Context: couch, after dinner, before bed, on a date night. They're not tech-enthusiasts hunting for features — they're looking for a fun, low-friction ritual that brings them closer. Both partners are present; one handles the phone, they pass it back and forth.

## Product Purpose

Closer is a couples guessing game. One partner answers questions about themselves honestly; the other guesses what they chose. Answers reveal side-by-side. Matches earn points; misses spark conversations. The goal isn't just to win — it's to learn something about each other, every session. Success = a couple finishing a game and feeling more connected than when they started.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, and elegant — all three in balance. Not saccharine, not cold. Feels like a premium date night accessory, not a quiz app. Voice is confident and knowing, not clingy or cute.

Three words: **intimate, gamified, tasteful**

Emotional goals: anticipation during reveals, delight at matches, curiosity at misses, warmth throughout.

## Anti-references

- Cold fintech / productivity dashboard aesthetic: sterile, data-heavy, no personality
- The generic AI-cream default: parchment backgrounds, rounded-32px cards, warm-neutral monoculture
- Cheesy couples apps: heart emojis everywhere, pink-heavy, saccharine copy

## Design Principles

1. **The reveal is the game** — every screen builds toward moments of disclosure; pacing and tension are the core UX, not information density.
2. **Earned warmth** — warmth comes from typography, pacing, and copy, not background color or decoration.
3. **One device, two people** — hand-off transitions, clear role indicators, and locked screens are critical UX infrastructure, not polish.
4. **Playful without being cheap** — game mechanics (scores, streaks, badges) should feel satisfying, never gaudy.
5. **Confidence over cuteness** — copy is direct and specific; no hedging, no over-explanation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text)
- Touch targets ≥ 44px (played on mobile, mostly)
- Reduced motion supported (reveal animations have fallbacks)
- One-device design: no simultaneous screen-reading requirements
